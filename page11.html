Instagram Account Security Guide

Understanding "CB Patch" Methods & Account Protection

Important Warning

The methods described on this page are forms of platform manipulation and abuse. They violate Instagram's Terms of Service and may result in permanent account suspension. Use this information for educational purposes only. We do not recommend implementing these techniques.

1 CB Patch Method (New Account)

This method involves creating a secondary account to manipulate Instagram's reporting system.

  1. Create a new account and follow your main account
  2. From your main account, report the new account 2 times for "Hate Speech"
  3. Report the new account 3 times for "Self-Injury"
  4. Wait 0-5 minutes for the new account to be banned
  5. Request to unban the new account
  6. The claim: After successful unban, your main account is "patched" against Community Guidelines strikes

Note: This method attempts to make your main account appear as a "trusted reporter" to Instagram's algorithms.

2 New Account - 2nd Method

This method uses explicit content to trigger a review and subsequent account restoration.

  1. From a new account, send explicit content in a Group Chat or DM
  2. Have the content reported with specific categories:
    • 1x Report for Nudity (Category 2)
    • 1x Report for Nudity (Category 3)
    • 2x Reports for Hate Speech
  3. Wait 0-10 minutes and check for review action
  4. If the account gets unbanned after review, it's considered "patched"

Note: This method is highly risky and violates Instagram's content policies severely.

3 Old Account Patch Method

This method is intended for older accounts that may have different standing with Instagram's systems.

  1. Option 1: Use Meta/Instagram logo as profile picture to trigger an impersonation ban, then unban
  2. Option 2: Get the account banned for spam 6 times (through excessive activity), then unban
  3. The claim: Future reports against the account may get stuck in review or result in "Couldn't Remove" message

Note: The effectiveness of this method is highly speculative and may result in permanent account loss.

4 Protection Protocol for Frequently Flagged Accounts

This is a set of practices for accounts that repeatedly receive Community Guidelines strikes.

  1. Immediately after unbanning, deactivate the account
  2. Reactivate during low-activity hours (late night)
  3. Remove profile picture
  4. Change username to a generic one like "Instagram user"
  5. Restrict DM settings
  6. Find and mute all Group Chats, then leave them
  7. Unrestrict accounts carefully but avoid engaging in chats
  8. Avoid changing profile picture or username for 2-3 days

Note: These practices aim to reduce visibility and avoid triggering Instagram's detection systems.
